# Iron Swing

A swing-thought checklist for striking irons — a sequenced set of cues from setup through follow-through. Part of the irons pillar of [[(C) Golf|Golf]]. These are personal feel-cues, not absolute mechanics.

## Setup → backswing → downswing

1. **Square the face at setup.** As you raise the club, keep the iron's face perpendicular to the ball.
2. **Start with quiet wrists.** Begin the takeaway by rotating the shoulders only — wrists stay resting, no early hinge.
3. **Hinge late.** Once the hands reach roughly 3/4 of the way to waist height, hinge the wrists and carry the club the rest of the way up.
4. **Don't lock the left arm.** A perfectly straight lead arm is wrong — let it bend slightly so the club finishes almost parallel to the shoulders at the top of the backswing.
5. **Invisible wall on the downswing.** Imagine a wall against the left shoulder as the club comes down (keeps the lead side from sliding/spinning out).
6. **Follow through — don't peek.** Commit to the full follow-through; don't look up to track the ball.

## Why these cues

The through-line is a **shoulder-led takeaway with a late wrist hinge** and a **slightly bent lead arm** — it trades the textbook "straight left arm" for a more natural top position where the club aligns with the shoulders. The "invisible wall" and "don't look for the ball" cues both fight the urge to come up/out of the shot early.
