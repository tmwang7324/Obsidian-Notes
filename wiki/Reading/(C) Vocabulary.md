# Vocabulary

Reusable terms and definitions captured from reading and daily notes. Filed under Reading.

## Social Security Act
A foundational U.S. federal law signed by President Franklin D. Roosevelt on August 14, 1935. It established a comprehensive system of benefits — old-age insurance, unemployment compensation, and aid to disadvantaged or disabled individuals — creating the bedrock of the American social safety net. Primarily funded through **FICA** / **SECA** payroll taxes: employees and employers each pay 6.2% of wages (12.4% for the self-employed).

## FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act)
The payroll-tax legislation that funds Social Security. Passed in 1935 as part of the **Social Security Act**; it formally took the name "Federal Insurance Contributions Act" in 1939 when Congress renamed the tax provisions that fund Social Security.

## Backlog
A pile of "things to do next" for a project — concretely, the `type: next-step` files sitting in that project's `Iteration Logs/` folder. The source `/daily-plan` pulls open tasks from.

## Derived Task
A plan item the skill *generates* (rather than pulling verbatim from the backlog) — e.g. when a project's `Iteration Logs/` is empty, `/daily-plan` derives candidate steps from that project's `Goals/`.
