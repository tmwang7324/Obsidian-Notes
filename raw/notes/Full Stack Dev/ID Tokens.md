
Tokens are pieces of data that carry just enough information to facilitate the process of determining a user's identity or authorizing the user's access to personal data or their permission to perform an action. All in all, tokens are artifacts that enable applications to perform the authorization and authentication process.

[Authentication vs Authorization]

Common identity frameworks and protocols use token-based strategies to secure access to applicaitons and resources. For example, we use OAuth 2.0 for authorization and OIDC (OpenID Connect) for authentication through external, trusted providers.
### Access Tokens
An access token is a short-lived credential that grants a user direct access to your resources and APIs.
