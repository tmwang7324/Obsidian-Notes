Cookies are small files of information (small pieces of data) that a web server generates and sends to a web browser. They function as digital memory, allowing sites to remember your login credentials, shopping cart items, and personal preferences, ensuring a seamless and personalized browsing experience. Essentially, they store a limited amount of user state

The cookies that are used on the Internet are called "HTTP cookies." Like much of the web, cookies are sent using the HTTP Protocol.
