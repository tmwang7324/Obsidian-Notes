# Key Takeaways
* A 401(k) plan is an employer-sponsered retirement savings plan with traditional (pre-tax) or Roth (after-tax) contribution options.
* For 2026, employees can defer up to $24,500, plus an $8,000 catch-up starting at 50 years old.
* Matching contributions from my employer can meaningfully boost savings, but many plans use a vesting schedule that ties the match to how long you stay.
* Early withdrawals before age 59 and 1/2 generally trigger a tax penalty, with limited exceptions.
* Review your contribution election yearly: limits, raises, and catch-up eligibility can all change what I'm actually saving.
* 


**Pre-tax contribution** is an amount of money deducted from my paycheck and deposited into a tax-advantaged account (like a 401(k), 403(b), or traditional IRA) before income taxes are calculated.

## Employer Match
An employer match means my company contributes free money to my retirement account based on how much I save.

For example, if my employer matches 6% (Barclays), then if I contribute 6% of my salary, then my company will also contribute an additional 6%.
