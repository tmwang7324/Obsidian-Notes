### Two pre-send checks
- **Apology-token count:** "sorry this took so long" + "I owe you a real apology" + "I'm really sorry for that" = three. The delay-sorry is the most droppable if you want it tightest. *(Optional.)*
- **Honesty check on "you let me into your life…":** strongest line in the text — keep it only if you mean it. You privately believe she withholds; if you write it as flattery while doubting it, the contradiction leaks later. If she genuinely shares things with you she doesn't with others, keep it. If aspirational, soften it.

## MUST SAY — drafting checklist

**Frame (open here, not the order number)**
- You owe her a real apology and want to get it right.

**Validate her hurt (all three)**
- You understand why it felt like distrust — especially right after the movie.
- You get why it hurt that your family scrutinized something she put real effort and heart into.
- You know she was trying to do something meaningful for you — and that's exactly what makes it hurt more.
- *(soft, optional)* You know hearing this after the fact may be its own kind of frustrating.

**Affirm her intentions — clean, full-stop, then proof**
- "I never doubted you were trying to do something good for me." (On its own. No qualifier.)
- Back it with concrete proof: 3 hours in line for Bear, Psyduck from when you met, the skincare routine, time out of a packed schedule.

**Own your handling (the actual fault)**
- You let confusion and anxiety get the best of you.
- Instead of telling her directly, you hid behind your parents' concerns because you were too scared to own yours.
- **One apology, landed on the specific harm:** made her feel judged by your whole family, with no way to defend herself.

**Forward ownership (close)**
- What you should've done was bring your confusion straight to her.
- That's on you, and it's what you'll do next time.
