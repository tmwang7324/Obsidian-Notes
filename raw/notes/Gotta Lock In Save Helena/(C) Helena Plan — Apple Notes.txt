HELENA CONVERSATION — BATTLE CARD
Reread right before you go in. The 5 beats are the spine. The boundaries, floors, and guardrails are the parts the in-the-room you will be tempted to abandon. Protect THAT version, not the calm one writing this.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PREMISE (locked)

• She's in PROTEST, not exit — the gifts demand was a bluff; when you offered to courier them, she backed off. She wants a reaction, not the stuff.
• Her "I don't trust you" is RELATIONAL — "you didn't have my back / you let your family put me on trial." Repairable through behavior, not argument.
• Your distrust is FORENSIC (the laptop story). DEFERRED to a later talk that the go-forward agreement will carry. Not buried — deferred, and named today.
• Outcome of this talk: reconnect + renegotiate how you communicate + TEST whether you can exist in this relationship un-exhausted. Not "repair back to the old normal."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

YOUR TWO FLOORS (private — for you, not her)

1. TRUTH FLOOR. You're giving genuine repair a real shot. But if, after real safety exists, she never owns the laptop or maintains she was truthful and you can't get to peace — that's a dealbreaker. The laptop talk gets a real horizon, not infinite deferral.

2. EXHAUSTION FLOOR. If, after you plainly state your needs, the standing cost of this relationship stays your daily exhaustion — that's a dealbreaker, independent of the laptop.

Both are real. Neither gets announced as a threat. They're the lines that keep you honest.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE 5 BEATS

1. RECONNECT — warm, not fake-cheer.
"Thank you for taking the time to meet me here. I've missed you — you look really cute today. During these few days I've thoroughly reflected on everything — on myself, on us — and I want to share my thoughts and get this right."

Optional, removes a weapon:
"And I'm sorry I went quiet after you called — I needed the few days, but I should've told you that instead of leaving you hanging."

2. SURGICAL APOLOGY — own the HANDLING, never the doubt.
"I really want to apologize for my cowardice in the car and when I brought you the Neo. I used my parents as a proxy for my own suspicion. Instead of telling you directly that I was confused, I dropped you into a situation where you felt judged by people you had no way to prove your innocence to. I also want to apologize for the times I prioritized my friends over you. My past actions weigh more than words, but I sincerely regret them and won't repeat them."

DO NOT apologize for HAVING had questions. You owe an apology for the shield, not the doubt.

3. ANSWER HER "I DON'T TRUST YOU" — directly, team language.
"When you said you don't trust me, I heard it. I can see how my actions made you feel I don't have your back. I want to earn that back. I dug into some of my most enduring, problematic thought patterns during this break, and I'm actively changing them, step by step."

4. THE GO-FORWARD AGREEMENT — your needs are co-equal, not an afterthought.
"One of my main issues has always been people-pleasing, and I've been finding that it's hurting me in this relationship."
(See boundaries below.) This is the TEST. Bring your boundaries with the same weight you give her hurt.

5. PLANT THE LAPTOP SEED — name, don't litigate — then close.
"There's still the laptop thing I don't think we fully worked through. I'm not bringing it up to relitigate today — I just don't want to pretend it's gone. When we're back on solid ground, I want us to be able to talk it through honestly."

One sentence. No "dealbreaker." Then close on forward-looking warmth.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

YOUR BOUNDARIES (beat 4)
State each as YOUR NEED + a JOINT ASK. Never as a charge about her behavior.

PRIORITIZE — limited boundary budget. Five boundaries lands as an indictment and drowns the repair. Lead with the two that drain you daily: E (time/autonomy) and D (lash-out). Keep A (over-referencing) — it's live in this conflict. If you can only land two, make them E plus whichever of A or D is most alive — but don't let D quietly slide off.

A — OVER-REFERENCING PAST MISTAKES:
"When mistakes I've already owned keep resurfacing, I shut down and stop being able to hear you — and I don't want to shut down on you. Can we find a way to revisit hard things that doesn't send me there?"

D — THE LASH-OUT / DISPLACED FRUSTRATION (one of your two biggest daily drains — LEAD):
"When you're frustrated — especially at other people or things going on — I end up the one it lands on. I want to support you when you're upset, but I can't be the place your anger gets aimed. Can we find a way for you to let it out that doesn't make me the target?"
   Anchor if she pushes back (don't lead with it): "Like when you were upset about the table and said everyone's useless, including me — that kind of thing sticks." That instance isn't deniable as a joke — which is what makes D your most grounded boundary, not your weakest.
   Hold the line clean: affectionate banter (stupid, fat) you enjoy = intimacy, leave it alone. Being the target of real anger meant for someone else = the boundary. Don't blur them — and don't swing to reading every joke as an attack.
   Fair to her: this isn't calling her abusive. Frustrated people snap; one moment isn't a verdict. It's "this lands hard on me — let's handle your frustration differently."

E — TIME & AUTONOMY (your other biggest drain — LEAD):
"Some days I need shorter contact, or an evening to myself because I'm not feeling great or I wasn't productive enough — and I need that to not mean something's wrong between us. Can we make room for that?"
   This is the one tangled with your exhaustion. The fear behind it ("she'll get mad if I limit our time") is anticipated, untested. Stating it IS the test: her reaction tells you whether the control is real and rigid, or a rule you've been pre-obeying that was never enforced.

YOUR MATCHING COMMITMENT (give this — it's earned):
"And I won't go silent on you again during something serious — a day at most, and I'll tell you when I need it."

WATCH-DATA (do NOT raise — observe over weeks):
• Popcorn / packed-food override — too small to state; citing it derails into litigating a snack. But it's the cleanest read on whether your preference is allowed to win. Private pattern test.
• The "nosy" jab — milder cousin of D. The sting was the jab-then-ask. Supports D, doesn't stand alone. Watch for recurrence.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DISCERNMENT RULE — don't make the verdict in the room

Her FIRST reaction to a boundary will probably be defensive — she's hurt, she just told you SHE doesn't trust YOU. A defensive flinch is NOT punishment.
• Don't FOLD to it (people-pleasing).
• Don't BAIL on it (over-correction).
The test isn't her face in that minute — it's whether, over the following days and weeks, she actually adjusts or quietly resumes the same standards. This conversation OPENS the test; it doesn't conclude it.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

EXECUTION GUARDRAILS — for the version of you tempted to fold

• Boundaries are written (above). Under pressure you can't improvise them away.
• Hold-line phrase: "I hear you, and I still need this." Then stop. You don't have to justify a need.
• "I need to sit with that" is a complete answer. Caving happens under pressure to resolve everything now. You can defer.
• Watch your body. The exhaustion / please-spike is your tell — the exact moment you're about to surrender a boundary. Name it to yourself and hold.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

CHANNEL + TIMING

• In person, or voice — never text. Text is what let the Ponyo/gifts escalation happen.
• At the end of your stated "few days" — honor the boundary, but don't let it slide into open-ended silence. You reach out to set it; that itself answers her unanswered call and the Ponyo text.
• Plan: short warm call to lift the break and SET an in-person meeting, then the 5 beats face-to-face.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE ONE THING: the hardest part won't be the script — it'll be not shrinking yourself in the room. You softened D four times in one sitting; that reflex will fire when she tenses. When you feel the urge to make a need smaller, THAT is the moment to hold the line.
