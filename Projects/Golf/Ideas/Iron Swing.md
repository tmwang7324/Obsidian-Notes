**TIPS:**

1. Position the face of the iron perpendicular to the golf ball when I raise my club.
2. Begin the swing with my wrists resting. Only rotate my shoulders to raise the club.
3. After I reach 3/4 to my waist, hinge my wrist and bring the club all the way up.
4. DO NOT KEEP LEFT ARM PERFECTLY STRAIGHT. In fact, it is necessary to bend it a little bit, so the club ends up almost parallel to my shoulders at the apex of my backswing.
5. Pretend there's an invisible wall against my left shoulder as I bring down the club.
6. Follow through. Do not look for the ball.
