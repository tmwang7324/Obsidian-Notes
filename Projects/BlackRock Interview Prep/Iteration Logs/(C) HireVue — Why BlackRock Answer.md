## The draft (~2:00–2:15 spoken)

> When I applied here, I wasn't applying to a bank — I was applying to a technology
> platform. Aladdin runs risk for trillions of dollars in assets, and what pulled me in
> is that it's one of the few places where the two things I spent NYU on don't pull
> against each other. I love building full-stack systems, and I'm obsessed with machine
> learning — and at Aladdin those are the *same job*, aimed at something that actually
> matters: helping institutions, and the pensions and savers behind them, understand the
> risk they're taking.
>
> I know that shape of problem because I've built a small version of it. I made a platform
> that ranks all 71 NYC neighborhoods for someone deciding where to open a business —
> embeddings to score how well each neighborhood fits what they do, a separate score for
> how saturated the competition is, Next.js and FastAPI end to end. Underneath, it's a
> decision-support engine: take messy, real-world data and turn it into a score someone
> can actually act on, when their own capital is on the line. That's a tiny Aladdin — and
> building it is the most alive I've felt as an engineer.
>
> Where I want to go, honestly: start where I'm already strong — shipping full-stack
> platform work — and earn my way toward the ML core. The piece that genuinely pulls me is
> the AI being built *into* the platform — Aladdin Copilot, and the NLP that turns
> unstructured text like filings and news into something people can act on. That's the
> exact intersection I love: machine learning wrapped in a real product. That's the
> mastery I'm chasing, and BlackRock is one of the very few places I can chase it on
> problems this real. That's why it's BlackRock, and not anywhere else.

## The 4 beats (memorize these, NOT the words)

2. **The mini-Aladdin** — NYC Neighborhood Analysis: data → model → score → decision someone acts on.
3. **Start strong, earn into the ML core** — full-stack now, risk-modeling / AI-on-the-platform over time.
4. **Why BlackRock and nowhere else** — the button.

## Accuracy guardrails (don't get caught wrong)

- **BlackRock is an asset manager, not a bank.** Never let "bank" slip out elsewhere.
- Keep the AUM figure vague — **"trillions of dollars in assets"** (BlackRock's own AUM ~$11–12T
  vs. assets Aladdin *oversees* ~$20T+ are different numbers; don't misquote).
- **Sentiment-analysis-for-profit is BlackRock Systematic Active Equity, NOT the Aladdin platform.**
  Aladdin's AI = Aladdin Copilot (gen-AI layer), NLP ingestion of filings/news, risk analytics.
  Don't pin alpha-hunting sentiment on Aladdin.
- Aladdin = **A**sset, **L**iability, **D**ebt & **D**erivative **I**nvestment **N**etwork.
