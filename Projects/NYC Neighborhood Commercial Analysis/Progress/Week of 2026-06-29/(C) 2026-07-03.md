# (C) 2026-07-03 — Honest framing decision + HyDE retrieval analysis

## Done
- Worked out how to **scope the project honestly** to interviewers: it ranks/scores/surfaces neighborhoods — never "predicts" — because there's no ground-truth outcome data; drafted the 30-second pitch and the "interpretable-over-black-box was a deliberate ML-judgment call" reframe.
- Analyzed **HyDE (Hypothetical Document Embeddings)**: it fixes the query→document embedding asymmetry by having Claude generate a hypothetical ideal neighborhood profile to embed instead of the raw business query; concluded it's a **relevance-quality** win, not a retrieval-scale one (71 docs = brute-force cosine is instant), weighed against extra latency/cost and hallucination risk.

## Decisions
- **Decided:** Position the product as a decision-support **ranking** tool, never "predict" — [[(C) Rank, Don't Predict|Rank, Don't Predict]]
- **Decided:** Rank with a transparent interpretable heuristic (embedding relevance + saturation ratio) instead of a supervised ML model, given no outcome labels — [[(C) Interpretable Heuristic over Supervised ML|Interpretable Heuristic over Supervised ML]]
