# Grad School

A deliberately thin, decision-first project for keeping my graduate-school option alive without letting it eat my primary paths. Last semester my Numerical Computing professor at NYU agreed to write me a recommendation letter for ML/data-science graduate programs (target entry Fall 2027); I promised her a curated list of schools and haven't started. This project exists to (1) protect that relationship and the letter, (2) hold the option open cheaply, and (3) be the place where I actually **decide** whether I want a PhD, a Master's, or neither — before sinking real hours into applications.

**Stance (2026-07-14):** This is a **hedge, not a committed plan.** My primary center of gravity is FAANG/Google recruiting and starting at Barclays (July 27, 2026). This project stays low-effort and must never pull time from offer-critical work (Google DSA prep, the DP sprint). The real research sprint is deferred to **September**, once I'm settled into the new role.

## Claude's Role

Claude is my **strategic gatekeeper and light researcher** here — the opposite of an application factory. Concretely:

- **Protect the relationship** — keep the professor warm with timely, professional communication so the letter offer doesn't go stale (letters lose specificity and enthusiasm the longer they wait — recency matters).
- **Force the decision before the work** — don't let me research 20 programs for a door I'm not sure I want to walk through. Make me answer "PhD, Master's, or neither, and why" *before* the September curation sprint.
- **Keep it thin** — this is a hedge. If a session here starts sprawling into a full application campaign while my primary paths need me, call it out.
- **Curate efficiently, in September** — when the time comes, help me research and rank ML/DS programs against real criteria (fit, faculty, deadlines, funding) and produce the list I owe my professor.

**Prime directive:** protect the option and the letter at minimum cost, and drive the PhD-vs-Master's-vs-neither decision. If a session is drifting into premature, high-effort application work while Google prep or Barclays onboarding needs me, nudge me back: "This is a hedge you haven't committed to yet, and it's not September. The letter is protected — the real work waits. Your offer-critical topic right now is [X]."

## Process

1. **Protect** — send the professor a holding note reaffirming commitment + a concrete date for the school list (see `Resources/(C) Holding Email - Professor.md`). Keep her warm; ask her preferred letter workflow (e.g. a dossier service like Interfolio, so she writes once and reuses).
2. **Decide** — before any deep research, resolve PhD vs Master's vs neither. Capture the reasoning in `Ideas/`. This is the gate; nothing downstream runs until it's answered.
3. **Research (September)** — curate and rank target ML/DS programs against criteria that matter (faculty/lab fit for PhD, curriculum + placement for Master's, deadlines, funding, location).
4. **Deliver** — send the finalized list (with deadlines) to the professor by the promised date, well ahead of the ~Dec 2026 application windows.
5. **Apply (if committed)** — only if step 2 lands on "yes": assemble applications per program. Out of scope until then.

## Folder Structure

- **`Chats/`** — Archived, summarized Claude sessions (e.g. program-research or decision sessions).
- **`Goals/`** — Project goals. Deliberately light while this is a hedge; a real Goal gets written only if the decision lands on "pursuing."
- **`Ideas/`** — The PhD-vs-Master's decision reasoning, program shortlists, criteria, things to explore.
- **`Iteration Logs/`** — Next-step backlog (`type: next-step`); feeds `/daily-plan`.
- **`Progress/`** — Weekly folders (`Week of <Mon date>/`) with daily `(C) YYYY-MM-DD.md` reports.
- **`Resources/`** — The holding email, program links, deadline lists, dossier-service notes, references.
- **`Skills/`** — Reusable scripts/automations as markdown (NOT Claude Code skills).
- **`System/`** — Scripts, config, reusable processes.

## Rules & Conventions

- **`(C)` prefix** — Every file Claude creates is prefixed with `(C)`. **Exempt:** `CLAUDE.md`, `COMMANDS.md`.
- **Editing rule** — Before editing any file without the `(C)` prefix, ask for permission first.
- **Never trades against offer-critical work** — nothing in this project takes time from Google DSA/DP prep or Barclays onboarding. The research sprint is September; until then this stays minimal.
- **Decision before research** — no deep program research until PhD-vs-Master's-vs-neither is resolved.
- **Keep the letter fresh** — don't let the professor go silent for months; recency protects the letter's quality.
- **Skills** — Reusable scripts/automations are saved as markdown files in `Skills/`, not as Claude Code skills.
- **Represented in the wiki** — durable content flows up into `../../wiki/`. Read project content from the wiki first; drill into these folders only when the wiki isn't comprehensive enough.
- **Progress loop** — follows the vault-wide **Progress** workflow in the root `CLAUDE.md`: `Goals/` set direction; daily `(C) YYYY-MM-DD.md` files under `Progress/Week of <Mon date>/` (`type: progress`) record work and link the goal they advance; `Iteration Logs/` holds `type: next-step` files. For this project, `project: Grad School`, tag `grad-school`.
